# Privacy Notice

## Hyacinth Medication Dispensing System

**Effective Date:** February 2025  
**Last Updated:** February 2025  

---

## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Notice explains how the Hyacinth Medication Dispensing System collects, uses, stores, and protects patient health information. This notice complies with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and other applicable privacy laws.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Patient Information

We collect the following types of patient information:

**Personal Identifiers:**
- Full name (first and last)
- Date of birth
- Medical record/chart number

**Contact Information:**
- Phone number
- Email address
- Physical address

**Medical Information:**
- Medications dispensed
- Dispensing dates and quantities
- Prescribing reasons
- Dosing instructions
- Lot numbers and expiration dates

**Notes:**
- Clinical notes related to dispensing
- Special instructions
- Allergies or contraindications

### 2.2 Staff Information

**User Information:**
- Name and role
- PIN (stored as secure hash)
- Activity logs

### 2.3 System Information

**Audit Data:**
- Login/logout times
- Actions performed
- Data accessed
- System changes

## 3. How Information is Collected

### 3.1 Direct Entry

Information is collected when:
- Staff enters patient information during dispensing
- Inventory is received and recorded
- Patient lookups are performed
- Corrections or voids are processed

### 3.2 Automated Collection

The system automatically collects:
- Timestamps of all activities
- User identification for each action
- System events and errors
- Security-related events

## 4. How Information is Stored and Protected

### 4.1 Storage Security

**Encryption at Rest:**
- All patient data is encrypted using AES-256 encryption
- Field-level encryption for sensitive fields (names, DOB, contact info)
- Encrypted database backups

**Access Controls:**
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Unique user identification required
- Automatic session timeout after inactivity

### 4.2 Technical Safeguards

- Secure authentication with encrypted PINs
- Audit logging with tamper-evident checksums
- Regular security updates and patches
- Network security protections

### 4.3 Physical Safeguards

- Access to workstations restricted to authorized personnel
- Automatic screen locks
- Secure disposal of equipment and media

## 5. Who Has Access to Data

### 5.1 Authorized Staff

Access is limited to:
- **Dispensers:** Can view patient information necessary for dispensing
- **Administrators:** Can access all system functions and reports

### 5.2 Access Principles

**Minimum Necessary:**
- Staff only have access to the minimum information needed for their role
- Access is granted based on job responsibilities

**Need to Know:**
- Access requires legitimate business purpose
- Patient lookup is logged and audited

### 5.3 Third-Party Access

- No third parties have access to patient data
- No integration with external systems without explicit authorization
- No data sharing with external entities

## 6. Patient Rights

### 6.1 Right to Access

Patients have the right to:
- Request access to their medication dispensing records
- Obtain a copy of their records
- Request an explanation of records

**How to Request:**
Submit a written request to the Privacy Officer. Response within 30 days.

### 6.2 Right to Request Amendment

Patients may request corrections to their records if they believe information is:
- Inaccurate
- Incomplete
- Incorrect

**How to Request:**
Submit a written request with specific corrections. Response within 60 days.

### 6.3 Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

Patients may request a list of who has accessed their information.

**Scope:**
- Access logs for the past 6 years
- Excludes disclosures for treatment purposes

### 6.4 Right to Request Restrictions

Patients may request restrictions on:
- How their information is used
- Who can access their information

**Note:** We are not required to agree to all restrictions if they interfere with care.

### 6.5 Right to Confidential Communications

Patients may request:
- Alternative means of communication
- Alternative locations for receiving information

### 6.6 Right to File a Complaint

Patients may file a complaint if they believe their privacy rights have been violated.

**How to File:**
- Contact the Privacy Officer directly
- File with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services

**No Retaliation:**
Patients will not be retaliated against for filing a complaint.

## 7. Data Retention Policy

### 7.1 Retention Periods

**Patient Records:**
- Retained for 6 years from last activity
- Or as required by state law (whichever is longer)

**Audit Logs:**
- Retained for 6 years
- Tamper-evident storage

**Staff Records:**
- Retained for 6 years after employment ends

### 7.2 Secure Disposal

When records are no longer needed:
- Secure deletion from all systems
- Physical destruction of backup media
- Documentation of disposal

## 8. Breach Notification Procedures

### 8.1 Definition of Breach

A breach is an unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises security or privacy.

### 8.2 Breach Discovery

All staff must immediately report any suspected breach to the Security Officer.

### 8.3 Breach Assessment

Upon discovery:
1. Immediate containment of the breach
2. Risk assessment conducted
3. Documentation of all findings

### 8.4 Notification Requirements

**Affected Individuals:**
- Notification within 60 days of discovery
- Written notice by first-class mail
- Content includes: breach description, information involved, steps taken, contact information

**Secretary of HHS:**
- Breaches affecting 500+ individuals: Immediate notification
- Breaches affecting <500 individuals: Annual notification

**Media (for large breaches):**
- Breaches affecting 500+ individuals: Prominent media notification

### 8.5 Mitigation Steps

Following a breach:
- Investigation of root cause
- Implementation of corrective actions
- Staff retraining as needed
- Policy updates if necessary

## 9. Uses and Disclosures

### 9.1 Treatment, Payment, and Operations

We may use and disclose PHI for:
- **Treatment:** Providing medication dispensing services
- **Payment:** Billing and payment processing
- **Healthcare Operations:** Quality improvement, training, compliance

### 9.2 Other Permitted Uses

We may disclose PHI without authorization for:
- Public health activities
- Law enforcement purposes (as required by law)
- Judicial and administrative proceedings
- Research (under specific conditions)
- Prevention of serious threat to health or safety

### 9.3 Uses Requiring Authorization

We will obtain written authorization for:
- Marketing purposes
- Sale of PHI
- Most psychotherapy notes

## 10. Security Incident Reporting

### 10.1 Incident Types

Reportable incidents include:
- Unauthorized access to PHI
- Lost or stolen devices
- Malware or ransomware
- System failures affecting data security
- Suspected insider threats

### 10.2 Reporting Procedure

1. Immediately notify Security Officer
2. Do not attempt to cover up or hide incidents
3. Document all known details
4. Cooperate with investigation
